PART 3

Right to Freedom(19-22)

Part III of the Constitution deal with Fundamental Rights. These are: Right to equality, including equality before law, prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex or place of birth, and equality of opportunity in matters of employment.

Article 19: Protection of certain rights regarding freedom of speech, etc.

*Every citizen has the right to freely express their thoughts and opinions through speech, writing, printing, or any other mode. This freedom is crucial for the functioning of a democracy, where citizens can freely discuss and criticize government policies and actions.

*Citizens have the right to hold peaceful meetings and gatherings without carrying weapons. This allows people to come together for protests, rallies, and discussions, provided they do not disturb public order.

* This gives citizens the right to form groups, associations, unions, or societies. It includes trade unions, political parties, clubs, and other types of associations to promote their interests and activities.

*In Simple Words: Article 19 grants Indian citizens the freedom to speak freely, assemble peacefully, form associations, move throughout the country, reside anywhere, and choose any profession. However, these freedoms come with certain reasonable restrictions to ensure they do not harm public safety, order, morality, or the integrity of the nation. These freedoms are essential for personal liberty and a healthy democracy.

Article 20: Protection in respect of conviction for offences

*Article 20 provides important protections to ensure that people are treated fairly and justly by the legal system. It prevents the abuse of legal procedures and safeguards the rights of individuals.

By prohibiting ex-post facto laws, double jeopardy, and self-incrimination, Article 20 prevents the state from misusing its power to unfairly convict or punish individuals.

Article 20 safeguards the freedom and rights of individuals, especially those accused of crimes, by ensuring that legal proceedings are conducted in a fair and just manner.

* In Simple Words Article 20 provides essential protections to individuals accused of crimes, ensuring they are not unfairly treated by the legal system. It prevents the government from punishing someone for something that wasnâ€™t a crime when they did it, stops them from being tried twice for the same offense, and protects them from being forced to confess. This helps maintain justice and fairness in legal proceedings.

Article 21: Protection of life and personal liberty

*Article 21 of the Indian Constitution is one of the most important and fundamental rights. It provides the Right to Life and Personal Liberty to every person. This article ensures that no person shall be deprived of their life or personal liberty except according to the procedure established by law.

*The "Right to Life" under Article 21 is not just limited to mere survival or existence. It includes living a life with dignity, free from any inhuman or degrading treatment.

* The "Right to Personal Liberty" means that every individual has the freedom to make personal choices and move freely without any unreasonable restrictions.

*In Simple Words: Article 21 guarantees the right to live a life with dignity and personal freedom. It ensures that no one can be deprived of their life or liberty except through a fair, just, and legal process. This right has been interpreted to include many essential aspects of a meaningful life, such as privacy, health, a clean environment, and access to legal aid, making it a fundamental pillar of individual freedom and justice in India.

Article 22: Protection Against Arrest and Detention in Certain Cases

*Article 22 of the Indian Constitution provides protection against arrest and detention in certain cases. It is a crucial safeguard for the protection of individual freedom and personal liberty, ensuring that no person is unlawfully detained or arrested

* Any person who is arrested must be informed, as soon as possible, of the grounds for their arrest. This means they have a right to know why they are being detained.

* The rights mentioned above (like being informed of the grounds of arrest and being produced before a magistrate) do not apply to cases of preventive detention.

* In Simple Words Article 22 protects people from unlawful arrest and detention. It ensures that anyone arrested has the right to know why they are being arrested, to consult a lawyer, and to be brought before a magistrate within 24 hours. It also provides safeguards against preventive detention by limiting its duration and requiring oversight. This article helps protect personal freedom and prevents the abuse of power by law enforcement authorities.
