PART 3

Right Against Exploitation(23-24)

Part III of the Constitution deal with Fundamental Rights. These are: Right to equality, including equality before law, prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex or place of birth, and equality of opportunity in matters of employment.

Article 23: Prohibition of traffic in human beings and forced labour

* traffic in human beings and begar and other similar forms of forced labour are prohibited and any contravention of this provision shall be an offence punishable in accordance with law.

* Nothing in this article shall prevent the State from imposing compulsory service for public purposes, and in imposing such service the State shall not make any discrimination on grounds only of religion, race, caste or class or any of them.

* The Supreme Court of India has issued several landmark judgments that expand the scope of Article 23, ensuring that any form of forced labor, whether direct or indirect, is curbed and penalized. The courts have also laid down directives for the rehabilitation of victims of human trafficking and bonded labor.

*In Simple Words: Article 23 is significant as it safeguards individual freedom and dignity, protecting citizens from exploitation and ensuring that every person is treated with respect and justice. It also reflects India's commitment to uphold human rights and eliminate practices that violate human dignity.

Article 24: Prohibition of employment of children in factories, etc.

* Article 24 prohibits the employment of children below the age of 14 years in any factory, mine, or any other hazardous employment. The intent is to protect children from working in conditions that are dangerous to their health, safety, and moral development.

* The prohibition under Article 24 applies specifically to employment in hazardous industries and occupations. It does not extend to all forms of work or employment. For example, non-hazardous work like child artists in films or children helping in family businesses (non-hazardous) may not be directly covered under this prohibition.

* The Supreme Court of India and various High Courts have played an active role in interpreting Article 24 to prevent child labor and uphold the rights of children. For instance, the courts have directed the government to ensure that children are not employed in industries such as matchbox manufacturing, carpet weaving, and mining, which are hazardous in nature.

* Article 24 is crucial in safeguarding the rights of children, ensuring their safety, and protecting them from exploitation. It underscores India's commitment to securing a safe and nurturing environment for children, facilitating their education and overall development. This provision also aligns with international commitments, such as the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child, which India ratified in 1992.
