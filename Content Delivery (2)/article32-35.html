PART 3

Right of Constitutional Remedies(32-35)

Part III of the Constitution deal with Fundamental Rights. These are: Right to equality, including equality before law, prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex or place of birth, and equality of opportunity in matters of employment.

Article 32: Remedies for enforcement of rights conferred by this Part

* The right to move the Supreme Court by appropriate proceedings for the enforcement of the rights conferred by this Part is guaranteed.

* The Supreme Court shall have power to issue directions or orders or writs, including writs in the nature of habeas corpus, mandamus, prohibition, quo warranto and certiorari, whichever may be appropriate, for the enforcement of any of the rights conferred by this Part.

* t acts as a safeguard for fundamental rights by allowing individuals to seek direct judicial intervention from the highest court in the country.

* In Simple Words: Article 32 ensures that if someone fundamental rights are violated, they can approach the Supreme Court to get justice and enforce those rights.

Article 32A: Constitutional validity of State laws not to be considered in proceedings under article 32

* Article 32A was added to the Indian Constitution by the 44th Amendment Act of 1978. It relates to the power of the Supreme Court to enforce fundamental rights and specifies certain conditions under which it can review laws affecting such rights.

* Article 32A allows the Supreme Court to examine whether any law passed by Parliament or a state legislature is in conflict with fundamental rights

* This article also ensures that the Supreme Court’s power to review laws is not affected by any limitations imposed by earlier laws or amendments.

* In summary, Article 32A strengthens the Supreme Court’s role in safeguarding fundamental rights by allowing it to review and ensure laws comply with constitutional protections.

Article 33: Power of Parliament to modify the rights conferred by this Part in their application to Forces, etc.

* Article 33 of the Indian Constitution allows the Parliament to modify or restrict fundamental rights for members of the armed forces, police forces, and other similar organizations.

*Article 33 allows the Parliament to make laws that can limit or modify fundamental rights for members of the armed forces, police, and other similar groups. This is done to ensure discipline and efficiency in these organizations.

* The article recognizes that these forces need specific rules and regulations that may require restrictions on certain fundamental rights to maintain discipline and effective functioning.

* In simple terms, Article 33 lets the government limit fundamental rights for military and police personnel to ensure they can perform their duties effectively and maintain discipline.

Article 34: Restriction on rights conferred by this Part while martial law is in force in any area

* Article 34 of the Indian Constitution deals with the suspension of fundamental rights during the period of martial law and the protection of actions taken under such laws.

* Article 34 allows the suspension of certain fundamental rights during a period of martial law (when the military takes over control due to emergencies or unrest). This ensures that the armed forces can operate effectively under such conditions.

* It provides protection to actions taken by authorities under martial law. This means that the actions of the military or authorities during this period cannot be challenged in court on the grounds that they violate fundamental rights.

* In simple terms, Article 34 allows for the suspension of certain fundamental rights and shields actions taken under martial law from being challenged in court.

Article 35: Legislation to give effect to the provisions of this Part

* Article 35 of the Indian Constitution deals with the power of Parliament to make laws for enforcing fundamental rights and the protection of such laws from being challenged in court.

* Article 35 gives Parliament the authority to make laws to enforce the fundamental rights guaranteed by the Constitution. This ensures that these rights are effectively implemented and protected.

* Laws made under Article 35 cannot be challenged in court solely on the grounds that they infringe on fundamental rights. This helps protect the legislative measures taken to uphold these rights.

* In simple terms, Article 35 allows Parliament to make and protect laws necessary for implementing fundamental rights, ensuring that these laws cannot be easily challenged in court.
