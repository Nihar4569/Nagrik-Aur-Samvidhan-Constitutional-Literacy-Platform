PART 3

Right to Equality(14-18)

Part III of the Constitution deal with Fundamental Rights. These are: Right to equality, including equality before law, prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex or place of birth, and equality of opportunity in matters of employment.

Article 14: Equality Before Law

*The State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India.

*No one is above the law, not even the government or its officials. Everyone is subject to the same laws and is entitled to the same legal protection

* Article 14 prohibits discrimination by the state against any individual or group. For instance, the government cannot make laws that apply differently to different people without a valid reason.

*In Simple Words: Article 14 ensures that everyone is treated equally by the law and has equal access to legal protection. It prevents any unfair or unreasonable discrimination by the state. However, it also recognizes that treating everyone exactly the same is not always fair and allows for different treatment where there is a reasonable basis, such as helping disadvantaged groups to achieve true equality.

Article 16:Equality of opportunity in matters of public employment

*Article 16 ensures that everyone has an equal chance to compete for government jobs, regardless of their background, thereby promoting fairness and justice.

* It protects against discrimination based on religion, race, caste, sex, and other irrelevant factors, ensuring that employment decisions are based on merit rather than bias.

* By allowing reservations, Article 16 promotes the inclusion of underrepresented communities in public employment. This helps to correct historical injustices and ensures that disadvantaged groups have equal opportunities to work in government jobs

Article 17:Abolition of Untouchability

*Article 17 declares that "untouchability" is abolished and its practice in any form is forbidden. This means that no one can discriminate against anyone else by treating them as "untouchable."

* Article 17 prohibits anyone from enforcing or encouraging the practice of untouchability. Any action or behavior that discriminates against individuals based on this outdated and discriminatory practice is illegal.

* Article 17 abolishes the practice of "untouchability" in India and forbids anyone from discriminating against others based on this practice. It ensures that all citizens, regardless of their caste or background, are treated equally and with dignity. The article also provides for legal action and punishment against those who practice or promote untouchability, making it a powerful tool for achieving social justice and equality.

Article 18: Abolition of titles

* The aim is to promote equality and prevent any form of discrimination or social hierarchy based on titles.

* Indian citizens are prohibited from accepting any titles from foreign countries without the consent of the President of India. This rule is in place to ensure that Indian citizens remain loyal to their country and do not hold any allegiance to foreign nations.

* By abolishing titles, Article 18 ensures that no individual is considered superior or inferior based on a title. This helps in creating a society where all individuals are seen as equal.

* In Simple Words: Article 18 abolishes all titles in India, except for academic and military distinctions, to promote equality and discourage any form of social hierarchy based on titles. It ensures that no one is considered superior or inferior based on titles, and Indian citizens cannot accept foreign titles without government permission. This helps in fostering a more equal and united society.
